Mission

On behalf of the Student Body, Illinois Student Government advocates for the betterment of our campus community with integrity, transparency, and Illini Pride.

In alignment with our mission to foster a supportive environment for all members of our university community, ISG events, programs, and services are open to all eligible persons regardless of race, color or national origin. Reasonable accommodations are available upon request, please contact isg@illinois.edu.

Branches

Executive Branch

The Executive Branch coordinates operational aspects, promotes initiatives, and speaks on behalf of the organization. It also facilitates relationships with university administration and external entities.

Administrative Officers

Administrative Officers
Administrative Officers provide leadership and oversight of operational aspects while advocating for students with the university administration, Champaign-Urbana community, and local, state, and federal governments.

Administrative Officers consist of the following positions:

Student Body President

  • Serves as the primary representative of the Illinois Student Body by remaining well-informed about students’ concerns and advocating for them.
  • Serves as the primary liaison to the University administration by regularly advocating for students’ interests.
  • Approves or vetoes legislation by signing their name or communicating their disapproval and rationale to the Speaker of Congress.
  • Manages external relations of ISG by communicating with relevant outside parties.

Student Body Vice President

  • Coordinate the Cabinet by sending a weekly agenda and facilitating the meeting.
  • Serves as the primary representative for internal relations by communicating with relevant internal parties
  • Assists the President with any delegated responsibilities.
  • Assumes the duties of the President upon their absence or request.

Director of Finances

  • Coordinate the development of the Budget and oversee all expenditures.
  • Provide oversight for all Illinois Student Government financial transactions.
  • Advise the Executive and Legislative branches on the use of financial resources.
  • Approve all expenditures.
  • Serve as an ex officio non-voting member of the Committee on Operations.

Chief of Staff

  • Coordinate the development of the Budget and oversee all expenditures.
  • Provide oversight for all Illinois Student Government financial transactions.
  • Advise the Executive and Legislative branches on the use of financial resources.
  • Approve all expenditures.
  • Serve as an ex officio non-voting member of the Committee on Operations.

Director of Administration

  • Oversee internal programming operations.
  • Oversees the ILIAD Coordinator, the Membership Coordinator, and the Programs Coordinator.

Director of Marketing & Communication

  • Facilitate communication with the student body.
  • Oversees the Communications Coordinator, the Marketing Coordinator, and the Design Coordinator.

Director of Community & Governmental Relations

  • Lead relations between local, state, and federal government entities pertinent to student life.
  • Oversees the Champaign City Coordinator, the Urbana City Coordinator, the Regional Government Coordinator, and the Civic Engagement Coordinator.
  • Attend all committee meetings of the Committee on Government & Student Policy.

Director of Student Life

  • Facilitate engagement with campus entities relevant to the student experience.
  • Oversees the Basic Needs Coordinator, the Campus Safety Coordinator, the Diversity, Equity & Inclusion Coordinator, the Health & Wellbeing Coordinator, and the Student Life Coordinators.
  • Consistently dialogue with the Student Trustee to ensure student advocacy is adequately addressed.

Coordinators

Coordinators
Coordinators and Liaisons are specialized positions that provide opportunities for students to contribute to the mission of ISG in specific ways.

Coordinators consist of the following positions:

Basic Needs Coordinator

  • Advocates for students’s basic needs and access to resources.
  • Serves as an ex officio non-voting member of the Committee on Student Welfare.

Campus Safety Coordinator

  • Maintains relations with the Division of Public Safety.
  • Serves as an ex officio non-voting member of the Committee on Student Welfare.

Champaign City Coordinator

  • Advocates for students' interests in the City of Champaign and attends all Champaign City Council meetings.
  • Serves as an ex officio non-voting Committee on Community & Governmental Relations member.

Champaign County Coordinator

  • Advocates for students' interests in Champaign County and attend all Champaign County Board meetings.
  • Attends the Savoy Board of Trustees meetings.
  • Serves as an ex officio non-voting Committee on Community & Governmental Relations member.

Civic Engagement Coordinator

  • Facilitates voter registration initiatives.
  • Facilitates events to promote turnout in elections among students.
  • Facilitates community service events.

Communications Coordinator

  • Maintains the ISG newsletter.
  • Provides official ISG statements and stances.
  • Maintains relations with the media.

Health & Wellbeing Coordinator

  • Works with administrators to improve student Health & Wellbeing.
  • Serves as an ex officio non-voting member of the Committee on Student Experience.

ILIAD (Initiative for Leadership, Involvement, Advocacy & Development) Coordinator 

  • Oversees and coordinates the ISG Leadership Initiative, a program designed to foster leadership and equip students with the skills necessary to succeed as student body representatives.

Marketing Coordinator

  • Maintains the ISG graphics and social media pages.
  • Take photographs in an official capacity for the ISG.

Programs Coordinator

  • Oversees coordination of ISG events.
  • Liaises between ISG and the Illinois student organizations.
  • Serve as a representative of the HOPE Scholarship Committee.

Membership Coordinator

  • Facilitates membership development initiatives and events.
  • Maintains membership records, alumni relations, and external representation.

Regional Government Coordinator

  • Coordinates with the University of Illinois and the University of Illinois System staff on initiatives that impact students.
  • Serves as an ex officio non-voting Committee on Community & Governmental Relations member.

Urbana City Coordinator

  • Advocates for students' interests in the City of Urbana and attends all Urbana City Council meetings.
  • Serves as an ex officio non-voting Committee on Community & Governmental Relations member.

Student Life Coordinator

  • Supports initiatives and projects related to student life, inclusion, and sustainability.
  • Serves as an ex officio non-voting member of an assigned Committee.

Technology Coordinator.

  • Maintains the ISG website.

iClicker Loan Program

The iClicker Loan Program, established in the early 2010s, was created to support students in accessing classroom response technology without an upfront cost. Today, the program is coordinated by the Jeffries Center and remains committed to reducing barriers to academic success by offering loaned devices to students who need them.

iClickers are available on a limited basis and distributed following internal program guidelines that center accessibility and student need. Devices are available while supplies last.

To request an iClicker, please get in touch with Brian Becker, Jeffries Center Assistant Director for Events, Outreach, & Digital Strategy, at blbecke2.

HOPE Scholarship

The Helping Others’ Personal Excellence (HOPE) Scholarship is a student-initiated fee that provides funds for students who demonstrate financial need yet do not otherwise qualify for financial aid. Illinois Student Government and I-CAUSE created and implemented this scholarship in 2020. This scholarship fund—a commitment by students, for students—is intended to help alleviate some of the burdens that come with attaining higher education.

Click here for more information about the HOPE Scholarship.

Teaching Excellence Award

The Illinois Student Government Teaching Excellence Award is an opportunity for students to recognize and thank excellent instructors for their hard work and commitment to the mission of the University. Any instructor may be nominated, from Teaching Assistants to Full Professors. Recipients will be selected and contacted in late March. Nominators and nominees will be invited to attend the Provost's Celebration of Teaching Excellence in April.

Student Governance at Illinois

A tradition since 1870.

The first student government in the United States.

In the fall of 1870 Regent Gregory was prepared to attempt a significant experiment in American education: the formation of a student government at the university.1
